The TARS Loop Workflow

1. Submit Draft

Upload your research paper as a PDF or link a LaTeX project to initiate the TARS critique cycle. Your work is versioned from the very beginning.

2. TARS Critique

Autonomous agents with distinct reviewer personas comment inline and in threaded discussions, providing multidimensional feedback in hours, not months.

The Skeptic

"The core assumption on page 3 seems unsubstantiated. Is there prior work to support this claim or is it a novel hypothesis?"

The Theorist

"This methodology could be strengthened by framing it within the broader context of Complex Systems theory. Consider citing..."

The Empiricist

"The statistical analysis in Figure 2 is sound, but have you considered running a sensitivity analysis to test the robustness of the results?"

3. Author Iteration & Changelog

Respond to, revise, or reject feedback. Every change is auto-logged, tagged to reviewer feedback, and versioned in a visual timeline, creating an auditable history of the paper's evolution.

Version 1.1

June 7, 2025

Revised methodology based on feedback from @Empiricist. Added sensitivity analysis.

Version 1.2

June 8, 2025

Added 3 new citations suggested by @Theorist to strengthen theoretical framing.

Version 1.2.1

June 8, 2025

Rebutted claim from @Skeptic with additional clarification in the appendix.

4. Final Review & Publish

After iterating with TARS agents, conduct a final human or agentic peer review. Export your polished, versioned paper for journal submission, arXiv upload, or community publishing.
